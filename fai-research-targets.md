## 3. Policy Decisions - Priority Targets

### TIER 1: CRITICAL (Immediate attention required)

#### A. Federal AI Preemption of State Laws
- **Status:** Failed in "One Big Beautiful Bill" (July 2025); Sen. Cruz pledging reintroduction
- **December 2025 EO:** DOJ mobilizing to challenge "onerous" state AI laws
- **FAI Position:** Supports federal preemption to prevent patchwork regulation
- **Action Items:**
  - Support Cruz legislation framework
  - Engage on DOJ litigation strategy
  - Counter state-level AI regulations (Colorado law delayed to June 2026; Texas TRAIGA effective Jan 2026)

#### B. CHIPS Act FY2026 Appropriations
- **Status:** FY2026 appropriations in flux; Section 48D tax credit expires 2026
- **Key Tension:** Trump called CHIPS "horrible" but GOP Senators view as national security critical
- **FAI Position:** Support semiconductor investments, R&D funding
- **Action Items:**
  - Advocate for R&D funding continuation after Natcast restructuring
  - Support Section 48D tax credit extension
  - Engage Commerce Department on new CHIPS R&D direct administration

#### C. SPEED Act (Permitting Reform)
- **Status:** Passed House 221-196 (December 2025); pending Senate EPW Committee
- **Timeline:** Possible Q2 2026 signing per Bipartisan Policy Center
- **FAI Position:** Strong support for NEPA streamlining (aligns with Techno-Industrial Playbook)
- **Action Items:**
  - Support Senate passage
  - Advocate for Special Compute Zones concept (from Tim Fist's playbook proposal)
  - Connect to AI data center infrastructure needs

### TIER 2: HIGH PRIORITY

#### D. Defense Acquisition Reform (FY2026 NDAA)
- **Status:** Signed into law; implementation phase beginning
- **Key Changes:**
  - Portfolio Acquisition Executives (PAEs) reaching IOC January 2026
  - Defense Acquisition University becoming "Warfighting Acquisition University"
  - Commercial integration emphasis
- **FAI Position:** Strong support; aligns with Techno-Industrial Playbook
- **Action Items:**
  - Monitor Army PAE implementation
  - Advocate for SBIR/STTR reauthorization (funding lapsed Oct 2025)
  - Support nontraditional contractor access

#### E. Open Technology Fund (OTF) FY2026 Funding
- **Status:** FAI requested $60M for FY2026 (House approved $43.5M in FY2025)
- **Threat:** Trump EO directing USAGM elimination; OTF at risk
- **FAI Position:** Protect OTF as exception to USAGM cuts
- **Action Items:**
  - Continue appropriations advocacy
  - Coordinate with State Department Bureau of Cyberspace and Digital Policy
  - Emphasize 46M monthly users, Iran VPN statistics

#### F. AI Copyright/Training Data
- **Status:** Mixed court rulings in 2025; no definitive appellate guidance until summer 2026
- **Key Cases:**
  - Bartz v. Anthropic (fair use for training, not for pirated acquisition)
  - Thomson Reuters v. ROSS (fair use denied for competitive use)
  - Appeals pending in 9th and 3rd Circuits
- **FAI Position:** Oppose maximalist copyright interpretation that threatens AI competitiveness
- **Action Items:**
  - Submit comments on Copyright Office proceedings
  - Engage on potential Generative AI Copyright Disclosure Act
  - Monitor class certification battles in 2026

### TIER 3: MONITOR & ENGAGE

#### G. Science Funding Reform (NSF/NIH)
- **Status:**
  - Congress preserved ~$8.8B for NSF despite Trump's $3.9B request
  - NIH getting $400M raise despite proposed $18B cuts
  - Indirect cost rate battles ongoing (15% cap vacated by courts)
- **FAI Position:** Support reforms emphasizing merit, reducing bureaucracy
- **Action Items:**
  - Support NSF Tech Labs initiative
  - Advocate for X-Labs concept from Caleb Watney's playbook proposal
  - Monitor Sam Hammond's ongoing Congressional testimony

#### H. Federal Tech Hiring (OPM/Tech Force)
- **Status:**
  - U.S. Tech Force launching Q1 2026 (1,000 early-career technologists)
  - "Rule of Many" compliance deadline March 9, 2026
  - Data Science Fellows Program launching Spring 2026
- **FAI Position:** Strong support for tech talent in government
- **Action Items:**
  - Coordinate with OPM Director Scott Kupor (spoke at FAI gala)
  - Connect fellowship programs with Tech Force
  - Support federal AI workforce development

#### I. Energy/Compute Infrastructure
- **Status:**
  - DATA Act of 2026 (Sen. Cotton) would let AI data centers bypass federal electricity regulations
  - PJM projecting 6GW shortage by 2027
  - Data center electricity demand forecast at 106GW by 2035
- **FAI Position:** Support Special Compute Zones, streamlined permitting
- **Action Items:**
  - Support DATA Act of 2026
  - Connect to SPEED Act and NEPA reform
  - Engage on DPA invocation for compute capacity
